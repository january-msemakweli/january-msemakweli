## About me

I work at the intersection of **population health**, **applied statistics**, and **public health systems**: observational analysis, reproducible pipelines (R & Python), and data-driven tools built for real-world decision-making.

- **Current role:** Research Assistant, Department of Epidemiology, **Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health** — reproducible R-based workflows for transportability and cross-context epidemiologic estimates.
- **Focus:** Causal inference, large-scale health data (claims, surveillance, climate), data systems for programs and community interventions.
- **Domains:** Climate & health, infectious disease, antimicrobial resistance, health systems.
